The Question

Strip away every power — who can actually play?

One trophy, sixteen contenders, no shortcuts. Read it like a tournament: settle the groups, sweat the knockouts, and find out who lifts it. No result spoiled until you get there.

✦ 01 — The Premise

No powers. Just football.

Every power becomes a footballing trait, and nothing more — gods defend set pieces like everyone else. Take the most powerful beings in fiction, strip what makes them powerful, and find out who actually understands the game. Three rules run the whole thing.

RULE 01

Attributes, not abilities

Every power becomes one football trait and nothing more — strength turns into aerial presence and a heavy shot, speed into a flying winger who strays offside. No flight, no magic, no jutsu.

RULE 02

Teamwork is a stat

The most gifted XIs have brilliant individuals and no shape — and a long group stage punishes that every time.

RULE 03

Temperament decides games

Ego and discipline are tactical traits: a bored Saitama jogs, Loki goes down too easily, and Zlatan only ever loses to gods.

The verdict

One quiet thing about football held all the way through: gods are strong, monsters are relentless, and one genius can carry you a very long way — but the trophy goes to whoever plays it as eleven. Konoha 11’s completeness beat the Z-Fighters’ engine not because they had a better player, but because they had a better team.

The thesis got proved twice over in the group stage, where the two most individually gifted squads in the entire field — the Justice League and the Devas — both crashed out without a win between them, undone by the exact same flaw: brilliant parts, no assembly. Eleven captains and a group chat, sent home early.

A thoroughly Germany-coded result, in the end: the disciplined collective, lifting the cup over the gods and the monsters.
